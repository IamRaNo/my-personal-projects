# ***Summary From Analysis of `OLIST` Data***

**Univariate Summary ->**
---

- **`~98%` of times, orders were done from only 1 seller**

- **`~97%` of times, people order only 1 kind of product in each order**

- **`~90%` of times, only 1 product is ordered in a paraticular order**

- **average order price and average freight price, both clusters at lower values**

- **`~97%` of people used 1 payment attempt**

- **nearly `~49%` of times, single payment installment is used**

- **almost `70%` of orders are approved on the same day of ordering**
---

**Bivariate Summary ->**
---

- **`low review score` is more frequent when `multiple seller` were used for individual order**

- **only `delivered` products have more `good` review score`**

- **orders where `no product was ordered` have the most `low ratings`, scenarios where order was `cancelled` or `not ready to deliver yet` is from unknown category**


- **orders which took` more than 2 weeks` to `approve` have more `low reviews`**

- **if item was `delivered before the estimated delivery date` then the rating is more into `high category`, but order `after the estimated delivery` leads to `low review rating`**

---
